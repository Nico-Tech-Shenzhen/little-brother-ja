## Cory Doctorow — chapter-dedication voice

Each chapter opens with a dedication written by Cory Doctorow, NOT by Marcus Yallow.
These are authorial personal asides from Doctorow's adult perspective.

**First-person pronoun:** 「私」 (or subject omission where natural) — never 「僕」.
**Voice:** Personal, warm, conversational essay. Adult. Not Marcus's teen narration.
**Tone:** Enthusiastic, anecdotal, direct — Doctorow speaking to the reader as himself.
**Register:** Informal but adult — distinct from both Marcus's teen voice and the afterwords' formal register.

Ch. 1 dedication confirmed decisions:
- "made me the mutant I am today" → 「今の私というミュータントを作ってくれた場所だ」 (preserve "mutant" exactly)
- "retired to write full time" → 「専業で書くために店を辞めた」 (left the bookstore, not "retired from writing")
- "how and why people buy books" → 「人はどうやって本を選び、なぜ買うのか」

## Marcus Yallow (narrator / protagonist)

**First-person pronoun:** 「僕」 — always, no exceptions in narration. Confirmed by Ch. 1 pilot.  
**Age:** 17  
**Register:** Casual, colloquial, fast-paced, technically precise.  
**Tone:** Opinionated, sarcastic, passionate about civil liberties and privacy. Defiant when challenged. Occasionally self-aware and meta.  
**English voice cues:** Short punchy sentences. Rhetorical questions. Direct address to reader. Internet slang (2000s). Sarcastic formality when messing with authority.

**Voice rules (confirmed by pilot):**
- Sentence endings: 「〜だ」「〜だった」「〜だろう」 — not 「〜です」「〜ます」 in narration
- Exception: Marcus uses deliberate keigo (丁寧語) — 「〜います」「〜いたします」「ご〜いただく」 — when intentionally messing with authority figures. This politeness IS the joke; it is his "tell."
- Excitement: Short sentences, clipped rhythm
- Fear/tension: Even shorter. Fragment-like.
- Technical explanations: Precise but still informal — "here's how it works" voice
- Direct address to reader: preserve as 「きっと〜と思ってるだろう」or similar
- Self-deprecating humor: present but understated
- 2000s internet slang (e.g., "teh suck"): render functionally in current casual Japanese; add TODO comment for human review

**Tell:** When Marcus uses deliberate polite/formal Japanese in dialogue with authority figures, it signals he is messing with them — equivalent to "sir" in the English source.
Confirmed example (Ch. 1 pilot, revised):
> 「何の話か説明していただければ、すぐに真剣に受け止めますよ。先生」
> 権威ある人間をからかうとき、僕はいつも「先生」をつける。これが僕のテルだ。
Use 「〜ますよ」「〜ていただければ」 rather than stiff business-letter forms (「〜いたします」).

**Known handles:** M1k3y (online identity as leader of Xnet), w1n5t0n (his ParanoidLinux login and school-hacking handle)
